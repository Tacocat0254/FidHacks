Company Research

Learning about a company before you apply or interview helps you tailor your application, ask better questions, and show genuine interest. Here's how to get started:

What to Research

  • Mission & Values: What does the company stand for?
  • Products & Services: What do they offer? Who are their customers?
  • Culture & Work Environment: What is it like to work there?
  • Recent News & Achievements: Any big launches, awards, or changes?
  • Leadership & Team: Who are the key people?
  • Financial Health: Is the company growing? (public companies only)
  • Reviews from Employees: What do current and former employees say?

Top Company Research Tools

  • Glassdoor – Employee reviews, salaries, and interview experiences
  • LinkedIn Company Pages – Company updates, employees, and news
  • Crunchbase – Company funding, leadership, and growth data
  • Craft.co – Company profiles, competitors, and news
  • Built In – Tech company profiles and culture insights
  • Comparably – Company culture and compensation data

Tips for Using Your Research

  • Mention something specific you learned in your cover letter or interview.
  • Prepare questions based on your research (e.g., about culture, recent news, or growth).
  • Look for red flags (e.g., high turnover, negative reviews) and ask about them respectfully.
  • Follow the company on LinkedIn and other social media for updates.
